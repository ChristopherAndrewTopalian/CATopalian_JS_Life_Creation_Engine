### CATopalian JavaScript Life Engine - Version 4 (Swarm Intelligence & Physics)

**Overview**
Welcome to Version 4 of the Life Creation Engine. This tutorial represents a massive evolutionary leap in the architecture. We are moving past simple random wandering and stepping directly into the realm of Swarm Intelligence and Physics-Based Interactivity. In this version, the digital lifeforms (nodes) are no longer blind to each other; they possess spatial awareness, and their emotional data directly influences their physical movement.

**Architecture: Data-Driven Physics & The Two-Phase Loop**
This script introduces two advanced concepts used in professional game engines and AI simulations:

* **Data-Driven Physics:** An entity's internal variables (like `this.compassion`) are plugged directly into its movement equations. The data literally steers the node.
* **The Two-Phase Game Loop:** To prevent mathematical glitches and jittering when multiple objects collide, calculations are strictly separated from actual movement.

**Core Mechanics Explained**

**1. Vector Mathematics (Attraction & Repulsion)**
Inside the `interactWith()` method, we calculate the exact geometric distance between two nodes. But more importantly, we calculate the *direction* using normalized vectors (`nx` and `ny`).

* **Repulsion:** If the nodes drift too close (`dist < 40`), they push away from each other (`this.vx -= nx * 0.05`). This prevents the HTML elements from stacking directly on top of one another.
* **Attraction:** If they are in sight but far away (`dist < 150`), they are mathematically pulled toward each other. This is the foundational logic used for flocking birds and schooling fish in modern simulations.

**2. Emotional Variables Driving Physics**
The genius of this engine is how the data ties into the geometry. The strength of the physical attraction is scaled precisely by the compassion stat: `let attractionStrength = this.compassion * 0.001;`. As the nodes spend time near each other, their compassion rises. As compassion rises, the mathematical gravity pulling them together becomes stronger. The emotional data is driving the physical engine.

**3. The Professional Game Loop Architecture**
Look closely at the `loop()` function. In simpler engines, you move a node, then immediately check if it hit something. If you do that with dozens of nodes, the ones at the beginning of the array get processed differently than the ones at the end, causing the simulation to tear or jitter.

* **Phase 1 (Calculate):** We run a loop to calculate *all* interactions, attractions, repulsions, and stat changes first.
* **Phase 2 (Apply):** We run a separate loop to actually move the nodes based on the final math.
This "Calculate Forces -> Apply Movement" structure is exactly how professional physics engines maintain stability.

**4. Multi-Stat UI Nodes**
Because we are dealing with a more complex social ecosystem, the constructor now builds and manages an Excitement bar (yellow) alongside the Compassion bar (pink). The `updatePosition()` method was upgraded to stack these bars cleanly beneath the node, proving that you can attach endless visual data streams to a single moving coordinate in the DOM.
