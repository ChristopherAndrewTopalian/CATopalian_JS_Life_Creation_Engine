### CATopalian JavaScript Life Engine - Version 3 (Slow Motion & Drift Mechanics)

**Overview**
Welcome to Version 3 of the Life Creation Engine. This tutorial focuses on the importance of fine-tuning the mathematical variables within a physics system. By making very small adjustments to the velocity and random drift calculations, we completely change the "feel" of the simulation, transforming it from a hyperactive bouncing space into a slow, peaceful, floating ecosystem.

**Architecture: Variable Tuning in Object-Oriented Design**
The architecture remains identical to Version 2 (HTML DOM manipulation), but this script demonstrates how altering floating-point numbers fundamentally changes behavior.

* **State (The Data):** The variables governing speed and drift have been drastically reduced.
* **Behavior (The Functions):** The movement logic now enforces a much stricter "speed limit" on the entities, forcing them to glide rather than bounce rapidly.

**Core Mechanics Explained**

**1. Slower Initial Velocity**
In the constructor, when the entity first spawns, its initial push is reduced.

* `this.vx = (Math.random() - 0.5) * 0.5;`
By changing the multiplier from `2` down to `0.5`, the entities start off moving much slower right out of the gate.

**2. Micro-Drift Adjustments**
Inside the `move()` method, the entity adjusts its own path slightly every single frame to simulate organic wandering.

* `this.vx += (Math.random() - 0.5) * 0.02;`
By dropping the drift adjustment from `0.1` to `0.02`, the entity changes its mind much slower. Instead of twitching erratically, it creates long, sweeping, graceful arcs across the screen.

**3. Strict Velocity Capping (The Speed Limit)**
Because the drift adjustment adds new velocity every frame, the entities would eventually accelerate to infinity if left unchecked. We use simple logic gates to cap their maximum speed.

* `if (this.vx > 0.7) this.vx = 0.7;`
* `if (this.vx < -0.7) this.vx = -0.7;`
By lowering the absolute speed limit from `2` to `0.7`, we ensure that even when an entity is wandering at top speed, it never breaks the peaceful aesthetic of the ecosystem.

**4. Smoother Compassion Scaling**
Because the entities are moving slower, they will spend more frames within proximity of each other when they do cross paths. To balance this, the compassion adjustment rates were scaled down.

* `this.compassion = Math.min(100, this.compassion + 0.2);` (Down from 0.5)
This teaches students that in game design, when you change one physical system (like speed), you almost always have to re-balance the interconnected logic systems (like stat growth) to keep the simulation stable.
